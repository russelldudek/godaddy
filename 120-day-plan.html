First 120 days

Earn the roadmap before announcing it.

An entry plan for a Director, Applied ML/AI joining a broad global entrepreneur platform.

Entry principles

Start with customer movement

Connect every technical conversation to an entrepreneur decision, action, or outcome.

Read before prescribing

Respect existing teams, architecture, experiments, and constraints before introducing a new taxonomy.

Make authority explicit

Agent autonomy, customer control, Guide authority, and production ownership are design variables.

Use evidence to sequence

The three-year roadmap should reflect dependencies, reuse, economics, and proof - not executive enthusiasm alone.

Technical credibility review

Architecture and pipeline

  • Data ingestion and feature/context availability
  • Training, evaluation, deployment, rollback, and monitoring
  • Online inference, latency, cost, and failure modes
  • Regional and product integration patterns

Agentic behavior

  • Tool access and permission boundaries
  • Memory/context and state management
  • Human review and Guide escalation
  • Evaluation suites, traces, incident recovery, and learning

Days 61-90 - Run bounded proofs

  1. Select one lower-risk and one higher-leverage entrepreneur moment.
  2. Establish current behavior and comparable baseline units.
  3. Run controlled tests with explicit authority and rollback.
  4. Instrument relevance, customer control, quality, economics, latency, operating load, and regional fit.
  5. Review weekly with product, engineering, analytics, Guides, security/privacy, and finance.
  6. Convert results into reusable components, documented limits, or a fast no.

Days 91-120 - Commit the three-year logic

Roadmap architecture

  • Customer moments sequenced by value, readiness, and dependency
  • Shared platform capabilities and domain-owned experiences
  • Evaluation, observability, governance, and regional foundations
  • Explicit investment, hold, and stop decisions

Operating system

  • Quarterly outcome and capability review
  • Weekly experiment and production evidence review
  • Incident, rollback, and learning capture
  • Team, partner, hiring, and skill-development plan

Illustrative roadmap landmarks

HorizonLandmarkEvidence gate
0-6 monthsTwo bounded customer-moment proofs; shared authority/evaluation contractObservable momentum, customer control, reliable operations
6-12 monthsReusable context, experimentation, and observability components adopted across multiple journeysReuse, faster trustworthy experiments, acceptable cost/latency
Year 2Cross-product next-best-move orchestration with regional controls and Guide integrationConsistent standards with local fit; reduced friction and rework
Year 3Adaptive entrepreneur platform learning across lifecycle momentsCompounding customer and business outcomes without authority drift

Success measures for the first 120 days

  • A shared mandate and customer-outcome vocabulary is used by technical and business leaders.
  • Two entrepreneur moments have explicit state, action, authority, proof, and freshness contracts.
  • Current architecture and operating constraints are documented without blame.
  • Technical and nontechnical stakeholders agree on scale/hold/stop evidence.
  • The roadmap distinguishes shared platform work, domain ownership, and local adaptation.
  • Known candidate capability gaps are addressed through technical review, hiring, partnering, and deliberate learning.

What Russell would not do

Announce a universal agent architecture before reading the system; treat engagement as proof of customer value; centralize every AI decision; automate sensitive exceptions without authority design; or present a three-year roadmap whose dependencies and stop criteria are invisible.
